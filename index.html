Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your information when you play Slash Pop.

iOS · App Store Android · Google Play
📅
Effective Date April 30, 2026

1Introduction

This Privacy Policy describes how Hest Studios ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use the Slash Pop mobile application (the "App") on iOS and Android devices.

By downloading or using Slash Pop, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

2Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide

Slash Pop does not require account creation, registration, or login. We do not ask for your name, email address, phone number, or any other personal identifier.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you use the App, certain information is collected automatically through third-party services:

  • Device identifiers — including the Advertising Identifier (IDFA on iOS, AAID on Android), used by ad networks for personalized advertising and frequency capping.
  • Approximate location — derived from your IP address (country/region level only). We do not access GPS or precise location.
  • Device and technical information — operating system, device model, screen resolution, app version, language, time zone.
  • App activity and usage — game events such as session start, session end, level reached, score, and ad interactions.
  • Diagnostic data — performance metrics and error reports used to diagnose crashes and improve stability.

2.3 Information Stored Locally on Your Device

The following data is stored only on your device and is not transmitted to us or any third party:

  • Personal best score, total coins, daily streak count.
  • Selected trail skin and unlocked skin progress.
  • Daily quest progress and rewards claimed.
  • Audio and notification preferences.

This data remains on your device. It is removed if you uninstall the App or clear app data.

3Third-Party Services

Slash Pop integrates the following third-party SDKs that may collect data as described above. Each provider has its own privacy practices, which we encourage you to review:

Google AdMob Advertising

We display advertisements through Google AdMob to support the free version of the App. AdMob may collect device identifiers, IP address, and ad interaction data to serve personalized ads.

Learn more: Google Privacy Policy · AdMob Privacy Information

Google Firebase Analytics Analytics

We use Google Firebase Analytics to understand how players use the App, identify popular features, and improve gameplay. Firebase collects app-event data and a Firebase installation ID.

Learn more: Firebase Privacy and Security

Apple Game Center iOS Only

On iOS devices, leaderboard scores may be submitted to Apple Game Center if you have it enabled on your device. This is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

4How We Use the Information

  • To operate, maintain, and improve the App.
  • To display advertisements and measure ad performance.
  • To analyze gameplay patterns and identify bugs or performance issues.
  • To comply with legal obligations.

We do not sell personal data to third parties.

5Advertisements

Slash Pop is supported by advertising. Two types of ads are shown:

  • Interstitial ads are displayed automatically between gameplay sessions (approximately every 3 plays).
  • Rewarded ads are entirely optional — you may choose to watch one to earn an extra life or bonus coins. They only appear if you tap the reward button.

6Children's Privacy

Slash Pop has the following age ratings on each platform:

  • iOS / App Store: 4+ (suitable for all ages)
  • Android / Google Play: 13+ (recommended for ages 13 and above)

The App does not collect any personally identifying information from users (no name, email, account, or login required). The only data collected is technical and statistical information through Google AdMob and Firebase Analytics, as described in Section 2. We do not knowingly use this data to identify or contact individual users.

On Android, in line with the 13+ rating, the App is not directed at children under 13. On iOS, where the rating is 4+, parental supervision is recommended for younger players, particularly regarding optional rewarded advertisements.

If you are a parent or guardian and have any concerns about your child's data, please contact us at the email below and we will address your request.

7Your Rights and Choices

7.1 Limit Ad Tracking

You can limit advertising tracking on your device:

  • iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → disable "Allow Apps to Request to Track" or, on first launch, choose "Ask App Not to Track".
  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → enable "Opt out of Ads Personalization" or reset Advertising ID.

7.2 Delete Local Data

You can delete all locally stored game data by uninstalling the App or clearing app data through your device settings.

7.3 GDPR / CCPA Rights

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or California, you may have additional rights regarding your personal data, including the right to access, correct, or delete data and to object to processing. To exercise these rights, contact us at the email below.

8Data Retention

Data collected by AdMob and Firebase Analytics is retained according to those services' own retention policies. We do not maintain our own user database or backend server. Locally stored game data persists on your device until you uninstall the App or clear app data.

9Data Security

All data transmitted by the App to third-party services (AdMob, Firebase) is encrypted in transit using industry-standard HTTPS/TLS. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10International Data Transfers

The third-party services we use (Google AdMob, Firebase, Apple Game Center) may transfer and process data in countries outside your country of residence, including the United States. These transfers are subject to the privacy practices of each provider.

11Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Effective Date" at the top will reflect the most recent revision. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
